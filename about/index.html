About me

I am a MSc student in Michael Hoffman's computational epigenomics lab at the University of Toronto. Previously, I obtained my HBSc from the University of Toronto in biological physics with a minor in mathematics. I am interested in 3D chromatin organization and how it contributes to disease.

In my free time, I enjoy creating bioinformatics videos, learning Mandarin, and teaching myself new programming things! My other interests include: musical theatre, running, piano, astronomy, and hedgehogs. Check out my CV page to learn more about my academic activities and accomplishments.
